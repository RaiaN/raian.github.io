Growth-stock screening framework

Apply this rubric to any IPO from the last 24 months. Scores are weighted; a name needs to clear all hard disqualifiers before scoring.

B. Qualitative criteria — 25% of score

  • TAM & runway (8%) — addressable market >$10B with credible expansion vectors.
  • Moat (7%) — network effects, switching costs, data, regulatory licensing, vertical integration.
  • Founder/management (5%) — founder still operating, prior scaling experience, insider ownership >5%.
  • Customer concentration (3%) — top customer <20% ideal; 20–40% acceptable with named anchors; >50% triggers a one-tier downgrade.
  • GTM motion strength (2%) — repeatable, measurable, multi-channel.

C. IPO-specific considerations — 15% of score

  • Lockup expiry (5%) — within 90 days = downgrade; 90–180 days = neutral; >180 days = upgrade.
  • Secondary offering risk (3%) — pre-IPO investor stake >50% with planned sell-down = downgrade.
  • Insider selling (3%) — material 10b5-1 sells by founders/CFO post-lockup = flag.
  • S-1 red flags (4%) — going-concern language, related-party transactions, dual-class super-voting, qualified auditor opinions, FX/CFIUS exposure.

D. Hard disqualifiers (any one = exclude)

  1. Pre-revenue or <$50M TTM revenue (excluding select biotech with Phase 3 data).
  2. Gross margin <20% with no path to expansion in 8 quarters.
  3. Auditor going-concern doubt.
  4. >70% revenue from a single customer for >2 years.
  5. Active SEC/regulatory enforcement action of material magnitude.
  6. Management has missed >50% of own guidance in first 2 quarters.
  7. Lockup on >75% of float expiring within 30 days.

E. Composite scoring

Sum weighted pillar scores; multiply by qualitative multiplier (0.85 to 1.15). Final scale: A >4.2, B 3.5–4.2, C 2.8–3.5, D <2.8 (sell/avoid).

About & deployment

What this is

A static, single-file IPO screener covering 50 high-growth companies that listed on US (NYSE/Nasdaq), Asia-Pacific (HKEX, TSE, NSE/BSE, KRX), and EMEA-ex-Europe (DFM) exchanges between May 2024 and May 2026. The framework, scoring rubric, and curated dataset are baked into the page so it works offline. Where supported, last-prices are refreshed live from Stooq on page load.

Live data sources & coverage

  • Stooq (no key, CORS-enabled) — covers US, Hong Kong, Tokyo. ~15-minute delay.
  • Twelve Data (free tier 800/day, requires key) — extends to NSE India, KRX Korea, and DFM Dubai. Add your free key in Settings.
  • For tickers no source supports, the table falls back to the as-of price baked in (showing a cached badge).

Click any row to expand full company details, GTM motion, and risks. Sort any column by clicking the header. Filter by sector, exchange, GTM motion, or score.

Deploy to GitHub Pages in 4 steps

  1. Create a new public repo on GitHub (e.g. ipo-scanner).
  2. Add this index.html file to the root and commit.
  3. Go to Settings → Pages, set Source to Deploy from a branch, branch main, folder / (root), and Save.
  4. Within ~1 minute, your site will be live at https://<your-username>.github.io/ipo-scanner/.

For a custom domain, add a CNAME file with your domain and configure DNS. The site is single-file, so updates are just one commit.

Optional: get a free Twelve Data key for India/Korea/Dubai coverage

  1. Sign up at twelvedata.com (free, no card).
  2. Copy your API key from the dashboard.
  3. Click ⚙ Settings in the top-right, paste, and Save.
  4. The key is stored in your browser's localStorage on your own deployed domain — never sent anywhere except directly to Twelve Data.

Caveats

  • Live prices are 15–60 minutes delayed depending on exchange. Use real-time platforms for execution decisions.
  • Baked metrics reflect Q1 2026 earnings season + S-1/RHP filings as of May 5, 2026. Verify against primary sources before any decision.
  • Indian FY runs April–March; some figures shown are FY2025 or FY2026 partial.
  • Not investment advice. Educational tool only.
